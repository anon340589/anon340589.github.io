Clock Example

Problem

I find it annoying knowing the exact time of day and obsessing over how I am spending every minute of it. I prefer the traditional round analogue clockðŸ•’beacuse of the rough approximation of time it gives you and because you can manually set it ahead so that you arrive early. But the round clock will not fit elegantly in the top status bar of my phone display or in the clock display of my desktop computer.

Solution

  • The line in the center represents the 30 minute mark.
  • The lines with a cross through represent the 15 and 45 minute mark. (Like when you count | one, || two, ||| three, |||| four, then add a cross for five).
  • Marking every five minutes like a round clock is too confusing in a small display area. So only every 10 minutes are marked with vertical lines.
  • Try clicking the clock.
  • To change the color, click and drag on the color bar at the bottom to choose the time of day, then choose the color using the color picker. 
  • Use the big slider to preview your color choices.
  • Use the small slider to set your clock ahead by 1-2 minutes so that you are always early. Clicking on the clock still gives you the accurate time!
  • Setting the color at different times of the day could be used to show sunrise/sunset/dawn/dusk, work/lunch/home/sleep time etc.
